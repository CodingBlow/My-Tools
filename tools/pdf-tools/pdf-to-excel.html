How accurate is the PDF to Excel conversion?
Our PDF to Excel converter uses advanced algorithms to accurately detect and extract tables from PDFs. The accuracy depends on the quality and structure of the original PDF. Well-structured tables typically convert with very high accuracy (95%+), while complex layouts or scanned PDFs may require some manual adjustments after conversion.
Is my data secure when using this converter?
Absolutely. Our PDF to Excel converter processes your files entirely in your browser. Your PDFs are never uploaded to any server, ensuring complete privacy and security. This makes our tool ideal for converting sensitive financial or business documents.
What types of PDFs can be converted to Excel?
Our tool can convert various types of PDFs containing tables, including financial reports, invoices, research papers, data sheets, and more. Both native (digitally created) PDFs and scanned PDFs can be processed, though native PDFs typically yield better results.

Tips for Better PDF to Excel Conversion

Use native PDFs when possible
Digitally created PDFs (not scanned) generally convert with higher accuracy. If you have access to the original document that created the PDF, consider using that for better results.
Check table detection accuracy
Review the table previews before conversion to ensure all tables are properly detected. For complex layouts, you might need to select specific tables manually for the best results.
Consider using CSV for simple tables
For simple tables without merged cells or complex formatting, converting to CSV might be faster and produce cleaner results that are easier to work with in data analysis tools.
Optimize PDFs before conversion
For scanned PDFs, consider running OCR (Optical Character Recognition) first to improve text recognition. Also, cropping the PDF to include only the tables can improve conversion accuracy.
