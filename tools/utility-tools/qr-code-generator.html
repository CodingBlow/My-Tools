Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a type of matrix barcode that contains information such as URLs, text, contact information, and more. When scanned with a smartphone camera or QR code reader app, it quickly provides access to the encoded information.

How do I scan a QR code?

Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes directly through their camera app. Simply open your camera, point it at the QR code, and tap the notification that appears. If your camera doesn't support this, you can download a QR code scanner app from your app store.

What's the difference between error correction levels?

Error correction allows a QR code to be readable even if it's partially damaged or obscured. There are four levels:

  • Low (L): 7% of data can be restored
  • Medium (M): 15% of data can be restored
  • Quartile (Q): 25% of data can be restored
  • High (H): 30% of data can be restored

Higher error correction is recommended when adding a logo or if the QR code might be printed in conditions where it could get damaged.

Can I track how many times my QR code is scanned?

This free QR code generator doesn't provide tracking capabilities. However, you can use URL shorteners like Bitly or custom UTM parameters in your URLs to track scans through analytics platforms like Google Analytics.

What's the best format to download my QR code in?

For digital use (websites, social media, emails), PNG or JPEG formats work well. For print materials, especially those that might be scaled to larger sizes, SVG is recommended as it's a vector format that maintains quality at any size.
